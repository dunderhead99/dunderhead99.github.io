Is it Shitted Upon or Shat Upon.

1:34 am, October 12, 2004

I don’t think I even have the right to blame anyone else. Everything that happens to me happens to me for a reason; and that reason is me.

See, normally, I’m the type of person who lets most everything roll off his back. I don’t vent my frustration on others and I never really have. It’s not in me, it’s not in my personality. I can be the most laid back guy you’ll ever meet. And, that could be the problem. No, that is the problem.

Now, I can’t really say I have a whole lot of experience in this realm. People, for the most part, leave me alone and I do likewise. Probably because they feel sorry for me, or they feel bad because I’m mostly a nice guy. Mostly. But, you know, every once in a while things happen like Saturday night; when people take it upon themselves to not just once, but repeatedly, and consistently, pee on my car in hopes to, I don’t know, cover the entire beast with their waste.

I can understand someone being so drunk that they’d do it once without remembering. But, when they go back for seconds and thirds and more, that’s not too drunk to remember. That’s malicious. And, you know, it’s disrespectful, but what can I expect? I’m not one who commands respect. And that’s my problem. That’s why everything that happens to me is my fault, even though I have nothing to do with it except for parking in the wrong spot on the wrong day.

If people know they can do these things to me and get very little of a response from me in return, then what’s to stop them from doing it again? They’ll just think that they can do anything they want and I’ll let it roll off my back and everything will be cool. And then they’ll push it a little further and a little further. And, it’ll all be justified. If I didn’t care before, why should I care now? Hey, urine can be washed away. Memories, especially mine, will dwindle and fade into nothingness in a very short span. All will be forgotten and all will be hunky dory until the next time someone decides to pull crap like this.

And, you know, it’s just a fucking car. Why should I care about a piece of shit car that deserves little more than to be used as a public toilet?

Well, I’ve been thinking about that. Because, it’s really a rediculous concept. Caring about material possessions is bullshit and I should know better. And I do know better. Because it’s not the car I care all that much about. I realize that now. You know as well as I that if we all (check that, I don’t even have to participate) decided to pee on someone else’s car, I wouldn’t give shit one. Fuck, I’d laugh right along with them. Because it is funny. It was a laugh riot when my aunt was changing my infant cousin and dropped her shitty diaper into someone’s convertible in the parking lot of the Tacoma Mall. What’s even funnier is to imagine the reaction of the driver, when they return and find someone’s diaper on their driver’s seat. Hey, no physical harm done, right?

I return to that lack of respect, that lack of respect I sorely deserve. I know for a fact that I’d never pee on any of my friends’ cars. Because it’s a reflection on how you feel about that person. If you can’t respect someone’s things, then you damn well can’t respect that person, especially if you know that they won’t do a damn thing to you in response. Not an equal response anyway. I know I won’t be doing that to any of my friends any time soon; even though I talk a good game. I’m not saying I’m better than them by any means. I just like to think I hold them in a higher regard than they do me.

But, you know, I’ll get over this. Fuck, I’m not perfect. I’m sure along the way I’ve pissed off my share of people. Not that my bong-fried memory could recall any of these instances at the moment. Sure, lines get crossed all the time. People move on. This is just me venting right now. It’s what I do. Writing it out will help me get to sleep tonight. And, tomorrow, I’ll wake up in better spirits. See, I feel better already.

It’s time to watch another O.C. Get ready for my interview tomorrow. Shave off this insufferably ugly goatee. When will I learn that these things to NOT become me? Then again, what ever does, am I right? Ha! Of course I am.

It’s true what they say. I really do feel a burden of some sort has been lifted from my chest. Maybe I’ll avoid that early heart-attack after all.
